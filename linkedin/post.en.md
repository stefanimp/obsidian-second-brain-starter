I have been refining my Obsidian workflow around a simple idea:

A second brain should not be a place where you store everything. It should be a system that helps you think, connect, and retrieve ideas when they matter.

My current approach is intentionally minimal:

- folders as workflow trays, not knowledge architecture;
- MOCs as living indexes;
- properties for retrieval, not decoration;
- Dataview/Bases for review;
- weekly review to stop the vault from decaying;
- templates that force clarity without creating bureaucracy.

I also prepared a public starter kit with clean templates, examples, Dataview queries, and a privacy checklist.

The important part: I would not publish a real personal vault. A real vault contains private context. What makes sense to share is the system.
