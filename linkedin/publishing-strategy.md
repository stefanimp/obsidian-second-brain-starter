# LinkedIn Publishing Strategy

## Goal

Position the author as a practical software engineer who can think in systems, communicate clearly, and build useful workflows with modern tools.

The target audience is not only Spanish-speaking contacts. The profile should become attractive for:

- Italian tech companies;
- international remote-first companies;
- engineering teams hiring in English;
- recruiters looking for clear communication, autonomy, and product thinking.

## Recommended Language Strategy

Use English as the main professional language.

Reason: high-paying remote tech roles usually evaluate in English. English content travels further across Europe, remote companies, open-source communities, and technical hiring networks.

Use Italian as a localization layer.

Reason: when moving to Italy, Italian content signals integration, seriousness, and local intent. It also helps with recruiters and companies that operate primarily in Italian.

Use Spanish as an identity and network layer.

Reason: existing contacts may be Spanish-speaking, and Spanish content can still perform well. But if the goal is Italy + international remote, Spanish should not be the only professional surface.

## Best Option for This Publication

Publish the main article in English.

Then publish two shorter companion posts:

- Spanish: personal/contextual version for the current network.
- Italian: localized version for the Italian market.

Do not publish three full articles on the same day. It splits attention and can look repetitive.

Suggested sequence:

1. Day 1: English article + GitHub repo.
2. Day 2 or 3: Spanish post summarizing the idea and linking to the English article/repo.
3. Day 4 or 5: Italian post with a local angle: learning, productivity, engineering workflow, and moving toward the Italian tech market.

## Profile Setup

LinkedIn supports profiles in multiple languages. Use that deliberately:

- English profile: primary for international tech.
- Italian profile: localized version for Italy.
- Spanish profile: keep it polished for existing network.

```txt

```

If you want a more job-market-oriented version:

```

```

Recommended Spanish headline:

## Positioning Notes

Avoid presenting the article as a productivity trick. Present it as an engineering workflow:

- information architecture;
- retrieval;
- metadata design;
- privacy-aware publishing;
- reusable templates;
- open-source starter kit.

That sounds more serious to technical companies.

## Suggested CTA

Use a low-friction CTA:

```txt
I published the starter kit here: [GitHub link]
If you use Obsidian for learning, engineering, research, or work, I would be curious to know which part of your system creates the most friction.
```

This invites replies without sounding like engagement bait.
