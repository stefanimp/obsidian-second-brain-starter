# Philosophy

This system is built around one uncomfortable idea: collecting information is not the same as learning.

A useful second brain should help you do four things:

1. Capture what matters.
2. Process it in your own words.
3. Connect it to what you already know.
4. Recover it when it can change a decision, a project, or a piece of work.

## Folders Are Trays

Folders are useful, but they are a weak model for knowledge. Real ideas belong to multiple contexts at once.

A note about public speaking may belong to communication, career, psychology, university, and personal growth. If the only structure is folders, the system forces a false choice.

This is why the proposed folders are intentionally boring:

- Inbox
- Templates
- Notes
- Media

They describe workflow state, not meaning.

## Links Are Living Tags

Traditional tags are quick, but they often become a flat list of labels. Links can do more.

A linked note can become an index, a definition, a conceptual map, and a place for manual curation. This is the role of a MOC: it turns a topic into a navigable entry point.

## Properties Are for Recovery

Properties are not decoration. They should answer practical questions:

- What type of note is this?
- What area does it belong to?
- What topics does it connect to?
- Is it draft, active, stable, private, or archived?
- What language is it written in?
- Is there a next action?

If a property does not help you find, filter, review, or act, remove it.

## Dataview Does Not Replace Thinking

Dataview and Bases are excellent for review pages, dashboards, and quality checks. They can find notes without an area, active projects without a next action, or deadlines coming soon.

But they should not become the main activity. A beautiful dashboard is useless if the notes behind it are copied fragments that you never understood.

## The Rule

A note belongs in the system if it does at least one of these things:

- clarifies an idea;
- connects ideas;
- works as a map;
- records something important;
- transforms a source into your own understanding;
- leaves a clear next action.

If it does none of those things, it should probably stay in Inbox, be simplified, or be deleted.
