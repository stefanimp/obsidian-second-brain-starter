# Workflow

## Capture

Everything starts as fast capture unless it already has a clear place.

Good captures:

- a question;
- a quote with source;
- a concept to explain later;
- an idea for a project;
- a task that belongs to a note.

Bad captures:

- copied paragraphs without context;
- vague links with no reason;
- notes that only exist because they were easy to save.

## Process

Processing means turning raw material into something usable.

Ask:

- What is the idea?
- Can I explain it without looking at the source?
- Where does it connect?
- Is there a decision or action attached to it?
- Does it deserve its own note?

## Connect

Use links in three directions:

- upward: to a MOC or broad concept;
- sideways: to related notes;
- downward: to more specific notes.

Avoid building a perfect taxonomy. Build paths you will actually use.

## Review

Review is the difference between a second brain and a digital attic.

Minimum weekly review:

- Inbox;
- active projects;
- ideas with no next action;
- notes in draft;
- notes without area;
- deadlines in the next two weeks.

## Evolve

Do not design the final system on day one.

Start with a simple structure and let pressure reveal what is missing. Create a new template, property, MOC, or query only when repeated use proves the need.
